Surya Namaskar (Sun Salutation)

Introduction

Surya Namaskar, also known as Sun Salutation, is a sequence of twelve powerful yoga poses. Besides being a great cardiovascular workout, Surya Namaskar is also known to have an immensely positive impact on the body and mind.

Steps

  1. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose): Stand at the front of your mat with feet together. Bring your palms together in front of your chest in a prayer position. Exhale.
  2. Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose): Inhale, raise your arms upward, and slightly arch your back, stretching the entire body.
  3. Uttanasana (Standing Forward Bend): Exhale, bend forward, and try to touch your toes with your fingers.
  4. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose): Inhale, step your right leg back, and bend the left knee while keeping the hands on the floor.
  5. Dandasana (Plank Pose): Exhale, step the left leg back, bringing your body into a straight plank position.
  6. Ashtanga Namaskara (Eight Limbs Pose): Lower your knees, chest, and chin to the floor, keeping your hips elevated. Exhale.
  7. Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose): Inhale, lift your chest into a cobra pose, looking upward.
  8. Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward Facing Dog): Exhale, lift your hips to form an inverted V-shape.
  9. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose): Inhale, bring your right foot forward, placing it between your hands.
  10. Uttanasana (Standing Forward Bend): Exhale, bring your left foot forward, bending down to touch your toes.
  11. Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose): Inhale, rise up and stretch your arms overhead.
  12. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose): Exhale, bring your palms together in front of your chest.

Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

Tadasana, or Mountain Pose, is a foundational yoga pose that promotes strength, stability, and awareness. It is often used as a starting position for other poses.

Steps

  1. Stand Tall: Stand with your feet together, and distribute your weight evenly on both feet.
  2. Engage Muscles: Engage your thigh muscles and lift your kneecaps slightly while drawing your abdomen in.
  3. Lengthen: Lengthen your spine, reaching your arms overhead with palms facing each other.
  4. Breathe: Hold the pose for a few breaths, focusing on stability and balance.

Vrikshasana (Tree Pose)

Vrikshasana (Tree Pose)

Vrikshasana, or Tree Pose, is a balancing posture that strengthens the legs and core while improving concentration.

Steps

  1. Stand in Tadasana: Begin in Mountain Pose, grounding yourself firmly.
  2. Balance: Shift your weight onto your left foot and place your right foot on the inner thigh of your left leg.
  3. Focus: Bring your palms together in front of your chest in a prayer position.
  4. Stretch: Once stable, raise your arms overhead with palms facing each other. Hold for a few breaths, then switch sides.
